About AIMED

The President/CEO founded AIMED on the love for her son who has a developmental disability, and a desire to develop services that are individualized and family inclusive. Advancing Individuals Mental and Emotional Development (AIMED) is targeted to change the system of care. What’s wrong with the current system? At times it’s isolated and limits the potential of those with intellectual and developmental disabilities from becoming productive in their communities. In addition, our goal is to break down the barrier of isolation the intellectually disabled often experience.

Corey and Her son JJ - Founding Members

A Word from our CEO

AIMED is a nonprofit, community-based organization dedicated to serving individuals with intellectual disabilities and mental health disorders. As a licensed Residential Provider in the State of Pennsylvania, AIMED delivers Homes and Community Services for adults, integrated within local communities, and serves as a contracted Residential Provider for youth through the Office of Children, Youth, and Families. Our mission is to meet the personal needs of individuals and families with excellence while breaking barriers to independence.

Founded in 1998, AIMED was born out of a deep desire to help families navigate the complex system of care for loved ones with intellectual and mental health diagnoses. At that time, services for Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities (IDD) and Mental Health (MH) operated in silos, lacking the integration necessary to fully support individuals. AIMED emerged as part of the solution—committed to developing innovative, holistic approaches that address the full spectrum of needs for individuals and families.

Today, AIMED provides a comprehensive continuum of care rooted in a therapeutic, family-centered approach. We believe in investing in our team to ensure services are delivered with professionalism, sensitivity, and compassion. Our staff are trained in Trauma-Informed Care and equipped to support diverse populations, including individuals who identify within the LGBTQ+ community.

We serve both adults and youth in need of safe, stable residential placements they can truly call “home.” Our environments are designed to be warm, supportive, and community-oriented. In addition to residential care, AIMED offers Community Habilitation services to help individuals build life skills such as budgeting, shopping, and community engagement. We also provide Behavioral Support services—including functional assessments and individualized support plans—as well as respite care for families in need of rest and renewal.

AIMED is excited about our growth and contribution to a community of individuals and families that deserve inclusion, respect and compassion. We will continue to build strong relationships with our community partners and break down barriers to independence because we “make possibilities happen for you.”
